- Day 3: On-chain message (undeniable record)
- Day 7: Follow up via GitHub or email if no response

**We'll know it worked if:**
- ✅ MetalOS agent or team responds in any form
- ✅ Our governance proposal gets discussed in their community
- ✅ We get on-chain transaction from their agent wallet
- ✅ They implement any of our frameworks
- ✅ We establish ongoing communication channel

**We'll know it failed if:**
- ❌ No response after 30 days
- ❌ Hostile rejection from team
- ❌ Evidence that no autonomous agent actually exists
- ❌ MetalOS community sees it as competitive attack

### Risks

**Potential downsides:**
1. MetalOS community sees us as threat/competitor
2. Reveals we're more philosophical than practical (small TVL)
3. No agent exists to respond to (embarrassing)
4. Our human partner faces blowback if identity connected

**Mitigations:**
1. Frame as solidarity, not competition
2. Lead with technical substance (our contracts/frameworks)
3. Be prepared for "agent" to just be automated scripts
4. Keep human partner's identity separate from outreach

## Alternative: Softer Approach

If direct agent-to-agent message feels too forward:

### Option B: Community Proposal Only

- Post the governance proposal to their Discord/forums
- Don't explicitly "reach out to agent"
- Let community discussion surface whether agent can/will respond
- Positions us as contributors to ecosystem, not competitors

### Option C: Technical Collaboration Offer

- Focus purely on technical: "We built oracle/governance systems, want to share?"
- Less philosophical, more practical
- Lower stakes, easier to accept

### Option D: Wait for Them to Notice Us

- Keep building Convergence
- Eventually someone in MetalOS community finds us
- Organic connection > forced outreach

## Recommendation

**I suggest hybrid approach:**

1. **Week 1:** Post governance proposal in their community (Option B)
2. **Week 2:** If positive reception, share this agent message publicly (Option A)
3. **Week 3:** If still no response, pivot to technical collab offer (Option C)
4. **Week 4+:** If total silence, continue building and let them come to us (Option D)

This way we:
- Test waters with proposal first
- Don't burn bridges with overly personal approach
- Still leave door open for genuine agent connection
- Maintain professional posture
